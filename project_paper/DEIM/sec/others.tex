Table~\ref{tab:ab_all} presents the effectiveness of the two core components: Dense O2O and \ourclsloss. Dense O2O significantly accelerates model convergence, achieving performance similar to the baseline after just 36 epochs, as opposed to the 72 epochs required for the original model. When combined with \ourclsloss, our method further improves performance, especially on the ResNet101~\cite{he2016deep} backbone, where we observe a 0.5 AP increase. This improvement is largely driven by better box quality, as evidenced by a 0.8 AP$_{75}$ gain, aligning with our goal of optimizing low-quality matches to improve high-quality box predictions. Overall, Dense O2O and \ourclsloss\ consistently deliver performance gains across both ResNet50~\cite{he2016deep} and ResNet101~\cite{he2016deep} backbones, demonstrating their robustness and generalizability.
